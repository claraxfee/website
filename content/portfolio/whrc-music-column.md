## About

A music column I started in early 2026 for [The Bi-College News](https://bicollegenews.com/) as an extension of my work at WHRC Radio, with the aim of building cross-organizational ties between the station and the campus press. The column covers live concerts, album reviews, and music news — focused on the Philadelphia indie and experimental music scene.

**Published pieces:**

- **[Sparseness & Depth](https://bicollegenews.com/2026/03/23/sparseness-depth-ear-at-the-warehouse-on-watts-and-juliana-barwick-mary-lattimore-at-the-first-unitarian-church/)** (March 23, 2026) — Reviews two Philadelphia concerts: electronic duo ear at the Warehouse on Watts, and Juliana Barwick & Mary Lattimore performing *Tragic Magic* at the First Unitarian Church. Explores their shared aesthetic philosophy of achieving maximum effect through minimum means.

- **[Art During an "Ambient Genocide"](https://bicollegenews.com/2026/04/13/art-during-an-ambient-genocide-engaging-with-christina-sharpes-shapes-of-grief/)** (April 13, 2026) — Engages with Christina Sharpe's essay "Shapes of Grief: Witnessing the Unbearable" to examine how musicians respond to atrocity. Compares Macklemore's explicit political gestures with Palestinian rock musician Rasha Nahas's approach of embedding political conditions directly into musical form.

## Timeline and scope
