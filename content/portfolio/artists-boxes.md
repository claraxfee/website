## Artist's Statement

Film is the only medium that can capture the processual dimension of textile art. A photograph embalms an instant; a film makes it live, because it introduces duration. *Artists Boxes* is built on that premise.

I filmed several people I know who work with textiles — some self-identified artists, some who just mend a bag or pick up a needle occasionally. I showed none of their finished works, only the making: hands moving, thread pulled taut, the sound of a stitch. Drawing on Bolivian textile artist Elvira Espejo Ayca's argument that the process is not merely part of the work but *is* the work, I wanted the film to honor what happens before the object exists.

The film is structured like a textile: cuts on a regular 30-second interval, fragments repeating and building the way threads build a weave. Sandra Monterroso's video practice taught me that placing ordinary textile objects in front of a camera transforms them — the bag being mended becomes, in the frame, an artistic object. I hoped *Artists Boxes* could do the same.

## Timeline and scope

Spring 2026. Made as a final project for "Into the Thread: Gender and Textile Artwork in Latin America" at Bryn Mawr College.

## Skills and technologies

Filmmaking, video editing, documentary interview, sound design
