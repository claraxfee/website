## About

A research report investigating generative AI tool use in undergraduate liberal arts education, focused on Bryn Mawr and Haverford Colleges. Co-authored with Emily Lu, Emma Yu, and Olivia Boyer under the supervision of Deepak Kumar, with support from NSF Awards 2023505 and 2336929.

The report covers three areas of investigation:

1. **AI-assisted coding tools** — experimented with GitHub Copilot and LLM-generated single-use applications, finding that code completion tools are unsuitable for introductory CS courses (they autocomplete full implementations of basic assignments) but potentially useful in software engineering contexts
2. **AI tools for education** — evaluated Google NotebookLM across six courses, finding it most useful for concept synthesis and citation work in seminar writing, and least useful for mathematical reasoning or deep close reading
3. **Student attitudes survey** — administered an IRB-approved survey to 228 Bi-Co students, finding a near-even split between users and non-users, with significant differences by school, discipline, and reasoning

## Timeline and scope

Academic year 2025–2026. Ongoing research assistantship under Deepak Kumar at Bryn Mawr College.
