## About

A senior seminar research paper in Spanish arguing that Lucrecia Martel's "Salta trilogy" — *La ciénaga* (2001), *La niña santa* (2004), and *La mujer sin cabeza* (2008) — expands auteur theory through sonic and haptic authority rather than visual control. Drawing on Jennifer Slobodian's application of Laura Mulvey's gaze theory, Dominic Russel's analysis of Martel's audio, and Deborah Martin's political readings of the films, I argue that Martel's consistent displacement of violence into the auditory space — what she hears rather than what she shows — constitutes a distinctly feminist intervention in a tradition that has historically privileged intellectual mastery and visual style.

Received the **Hester-Ann Corner Prize for Literatures in Spanish** at Bryn Mawr College.
